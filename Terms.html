The Adventurer

The Adventuring Dictionary

Term Definition
Ablility Skill Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Wisdom, Intelligence, Charisma; used to show how strong/smart (etc) you are when trying to accomplish pertanint task
Armor Class The number the attacker must roll higher than to hit; Armor Type + Dex mod + Misc (Shield/Enchantments)
Class The specific training and skills that the characters have; examples of classes: Barbarian, Bard, Cleric, Fighter, Ranger, Rogue, Sorccer, Warlock, and Wizard
d20 A dice that has 20 sides with numbers 1-20; used for any abililty checks, attack rolls, or saving throws
DM Stands for Dungeon Master; The Narrator of the Quest; Plays as all NPC; aka Game Master (GM)
Hit Points The amount of damage you can take before you faint/die
Homebrew A quest or world which the DM makes up
Mary Sue The perfect character; little to no flaws; perfectly bueatiful/handsom; some type of extreme powers; too god to be true
Melee Weapons or attacks that are on close quarters, hand to hand; examples: swords, battleaxes, quarterstaffs
NPC Stands for Non-Player Characters; All extra characters in the story but played by the DM
Party The group of the other players' characters that you are playing with, and hopefully working together with; NEVER split the party!!!
Player's Handbook This is the base set of rules for D&D; has step by step of creating characters, taking different actions, and casting spells
Race The main races players can choose to be are Elves, Dwarfs, Gnomes, Orcs, and Humans; each race gives unique skills and abilities
Spell Casting Focus A iten used to channel magical engery; examples: Wands, Staffs, Amulets, Rings, (etc); replaces componets spells require
Spell Slots The amount of magic that the user can cast inbetween rests; higher slots caster stronger spells; magical stamina
